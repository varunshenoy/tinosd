Welcome to the Cupertino Speech Debate Summer Camp

With Cupertino Speech and Debate ranking 5th in the Coast Forensic League, it is one of the most competitive, high-achieving and largest student club in Cupertino High School. This summer, Cupertino High School will be hosting its very own summer camp taught by qualified student captains. This camp will be open to incoming 8th and 9th graders, for both novice and experienced students. The camp offers three events including Congressional debate, Impromptu speech and Extemporaneous speech (event descriptions can be seen below), however students will only be participating and competing in one event of their choice. Throughout the three days, students will learn and train in a specific event through lectures, drills, small group lab meetings, and watching demo speeches.

Events Offered

Impromptu Speaking

Impromptu is a public speaking event where students have three minutes to select a topic, brainstorm their ideas, outline the speech, and finally, deliver the speech. The speech is given without notes and uses an introduction, body, and conclusion. The speech can be light-hearted or serious. The speech can be based upon prompts that range from nursery rhymes, current events, celebrities, organizations, and more.

Extemporaneous Speaking

Students are presented with a choice of three questions related to current events, in 30 minutes, prepare a seven-minute speech answering the selected question. Students may consult articles and evidence they gather prior to the contest, but may not use the Internet during preparation. Topics range from political matters to economic concerns to foreign policy. The speech is delivered from memory.

Congressional Debate

A simulation of the U.S. legislative process, students generate a series of bills and resolutions for debate in Congressional Debate. Debaters alternate delivering speeches for and against the topic in a group setting. An elected student serves as a presiding officer to ensure debate flows smoothly. Students are assessed on their research, argumentation, and delivery skills, as well as their knowledge and use of parliamentary procedure.

Instructors

How We Teach

Cupertino High School’s student instructors have well-equipped experience through their qualifications to various nationally recognized tournaments, and are more than equipped to pass their tactics and expertise. Our student captains have led Cupertino Speech and Debate to sweep, meaning winning tournaments overall, in MLK 2 years in a row, SCU and UOP. These students have achieved elimination and final rounds at many prestigious national tournaments such as Berkeley, Stanford, ASU, UoP, Nat Quals, and so on. With the help of student captains, our club was able to expand to 200 competitors in the last year alone, allowing them to not only enhance their experience but to win hundreds of awards. Our instructors have planned hourly curriculums that will enhance and develop your child’s speaking and presenting skills as well as prepare them for future competitions.
